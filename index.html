My Backgrount and Why I Want to Become a Web Developer

After I got my bachelor's degree in physics, I decided to make a career shift due to the lack of jobs in this field in Gaza. I worked as a managerial assistant, a teacher, and a freelance writer. Every one of these jobs has taught me new things and enhanced my skills in some areas. And although I was successful in almost every single job I worked on, I wanted to start a career where I can grow, develop new skills, achieve my potential, and most importantly, make a positive impact on my community and build useful things. And in the same time, I wanted a high-paying job that will fulfil my financial commitments. Furthermore, I've been always passionate about working in the STEM field. I liked to solve problems, provide solutions, and build complex things. One example of that is my graduation project which was to write a code to analyze data from the LHC at CERN to try to find new subatomic particles. I really enjoyed working on that project, and I even self-learned python to be able to finish it.
